IMU Data Extraction for Hyperloop Pod


Kalman Filter

Learning about the Kalman Filter is one of the most interesting projects I have ever landed on. The goal is to use the onboard IMU and extract the necessary kinematic measurements (position, velocity, acceleration). The challenge is that the IMU will tilt, which corrupts the acceleration readings due to the acceleration of gravity, and the IMU is particularly noisy and will drift over time.


In the above image, the plot on the left is the acceleration in the x, y, and z axis of test data created using only angular velocity data and quaternion rotations (the dataset involved only a 90 degree rotation about an axis). The plot on the right is the acceleration while the IMU is at rest. The noticeable drift illustrates the consequences of floating point error and noise created in iterative computation. These are things we aim to resolve in order to get an accurate measure of velocity from the IMU.


Above are two plots comparing raw IMU acceleration data to a Kalman Filtered version. The tested dataset was a series of linear movements along an axis. There is noticeable bias or perhaps static offset with the filtered version. I am continuing to look into methods to tune Q in the prediction step.
